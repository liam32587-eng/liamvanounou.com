WEEK 1 & 2

Foundation

Building the base ✓ Unlocked

Day 1 — Push & Core

Incline Push Up
3 × 10
+
1
Place hands on an elevated surface (chair, step, wall). Wider than shoulder-width.
2
Lower your chest toward the surface with elbows at roughly 45°.
3
Push back to start with full arm extension. Keep your body in a straight line the whole time.
Keep your body completely straight — head to heels, like a plank
Squeeze your chest at the top of every rep for 1 second
Don't flare your elbows out to 90° — they should point back at about 45°
Hollow Body Hold
3 × 20 sec
+
1
Lie on your back. Press your lower back firmly into the floor — this must stay down the whole time.
2
Raise your shoulders slightly off the floor. Lift your legs (start with knees bent if needed).
3
Hold the position. Breathe steadily — imagine pulling your belly button to your spine.
Lower back must stay flat — if it arches, bend your knees more
Keep breathing — holding your breath makes it collapse faster
Don't just lift your legs and hope for the best — the lower back contact is the whole exercise
Plank
3 × 30 sec
+
1
Forearms on floor, elbows directly under shoulders. Toes tucked.
2
Lift into a straight line from head to heels. Squeeze your glutes and brace your core.
3
Hold for the full time. Think "push the floor away" with your forearms.
Squeeze glutes, quads, and core at the same time — all three
Breathe steadily — a shaky breathing plank beats a held-breath plank
Don't let your hips sag down OR shoot up — keep the line perfectly straight
Pike Push Up
3 × 8
+
1
Start in downward dog — hips high, hands and feet on floor, forming an inverted V.
2
Bend your elbows and lower your head toward the floor between your hands.
3
Press back up to start. Hips stay high throughout the whole movement.
The higher your hips, the more shoulder load — don't let them drop
Elbows track over your wrists as you lower — don't flare them out
This is not a push up — don't lower your chest. Your head goes toward the floor.
Leg Raises
3 × 10
+
1
Lie flat on your back. Place hands under your lower back for support.
2
Raise both legs together to 90° with slow control.
3
Lower slowly — stop 2 inches off the floor, then raise again. Don't touch down.
Slow the lowering down — that's where the core work actually happens
Keep your neck relaxed — don't crane your head up
Don't use momentum — if you're swinging, slow down and do fewer reps with control

Day 2 — Squat & Hinge

Bodyweight Squat
4 × 15
+
1
Feet shoulder-width apart, toes turned slightly out. Arms forward for balance.
2
Sit back and down — like sitting into a chair. Keep your chest up.
3
Drive through your heels to stand. Squeeze glutes at the top.
Knees track over your toes — don't let them cave inward
Go as deep as your mobility allows — full depth over time is the goal
Don't let your heels rise off the floor — push through your whole foot
Glute Bridge
3 × 15
+
1
Lie on your back, feet flat near your hips, arms by your sides.
2
Drive hips up by squeezing your glutes. Form a straight line from knees to shoulders.
3
Squeeze hard at the top for 1 second. Lower slowly with control.
The squeeze at the top is the point — don't rush through it
Drive through your heels, not your toes
Don't hyperextend your lower back at the top — tuck your pelvis slightly
Reverse Lunge
3 × 10 each
+
1
Stand tall. Step one foot directly behind you.
2
Lower your back knee toward the floor. Keep your torso upright.
3
Push through your front heel to return to standing. Repeat on same leg for the set.
Front knee stays stacked over your ankle — don't let it drift forward
Torso stays straight — don't lean forward into your thigh
Don't let your front knee collapse inward — that's your glutes not firing
Good Morning
3 × 12
+
1
Stand with hands behind your head and a slight bend in your knees.
2
Hinge at your hips — push them backward while keeping your back flat.
3
Return to standing by driving your hips forward. Feel the hamstrings stretch on the way down.
This is a hip hinge — not a squat. Hips go back, not down.
Keep your back flat — no rounding at the bottom
Don't round your spine — if your back rounds, you've gone too far. Reduce the range.
Calf Raise
3 × 20
+
1
Stand with feet hip-width apart. Hold a wall for balance if needed.
2
Rise up onto the balls of your feet as high as possible.
3
Lower slowly to full stretch. Pause briefly at top and bottom.
Slow is better than fast — 1 second up, 2 seconds down
Use a step edge for more range once this feels easy
Don't bounce — if you're bouncing you're not building strength, just momentum

Day 3 — Pull & Shoulders

Superman Hold
3 × 20 sec
+
1
Lie face down with arms extended overhead and legs straight.
2
Lift your arms, chest, and legs off the floor simultaneously.
3
Squeeze everything and hold. Breathe steadily.
Lift from your chest — don't just crane your neck up
Squeeze your glutes — they hold the leg lift
Don't only lift your arms — the legs must come up equally for this to count
Arch Body Hold
3 × 20 sec
+
1
Lie face down. Arms can be overhead (harder) or by your sides (easier).
2
Lift your arms, chest, and legs — forming a banana shape.
3
Hold the position and breathe. Squeeze glutes and upper back throughout.
This is the exact counterpart to hollow body — train both equally
The arch in gymnastics is a controlled position — not just a sag
Don't collapse between sets — reset fully before each hold
Prone Y-T-W
3 × 8 each
+
1
Lie face down. Form a Y shape — arms overhead in a V. Lift and squeeze. Lower.
2
Form a T — arms straight out to sides. Lift and squeeze. Lower.
3
Form a W — elbows bent, like a flex. Lift and squeeze. Lower. 8 reps each letter.
No weight needed — your arms are enough for this muscle group
Focus on squeezing the shoulder blades together on every single rep
Don't rush — these are small muscles doing important work. Slow and deliberate.
Table Row
3 × 10
+
1
Lie under a sturdy table. Grip the edge with an overhand grip.
2
Keep your body straight — like a reverse plank. Pull your chest up to the table.
3
Lower with full control. Repeat.
Squeeze your shoulder blades hard at the top of every rep
Don't let your hips sag — keep the body rigid throughout
Don't pull with just your arms — drive your elbows back and down
Shoulder Tap Plank
3 × 10 each
+
1
Get into a high plank — arms straight, hands under shoulders.
2
Brace hard, then lift one hand and tap the opposite shoulder.
3
Replace the hand and repeat on the other side. Alternate for 10 per side.
Brace your core before every single tap — don't wait until you're already rotating
Slow is better — the goal is zero hip rotation, not fast reps
Don't let your hips rotate side-to-side — if they are, widen your feet or slow down
WEEK 3 & 4

Build

Adding volume & difficulty Unlocked

Day 1 — Push & Core

Full Push Up
4 × 10
+
1
Place hands slightly wider than shoulder-width on the floor. Body in a rigid line from head to heels.
2
Lower your chest to the floor with elbows at 45° from your body — not flared out.
3
Press back up to full lockout. Squeeze your chest at the top for one second before the next rep.
If you can't complete 10 with good form, drop to 6 and build — never sacrifice the plank line for reps
Think "push the floor away" rather than "lower yourself down" — it changes how you engage
Don't let your hips pike up as you fatigue — this is the most common form breakdown and makes the push up useless
Hollow Body Hold
3 × 30 sec
+
1
Lie on your back. Press your lower back into the floor and don't let it leave throughout the hold.
2
Raise shoulders and legs. Arms can be overhead (harder) or by your sides (slightly easier).
3
Hold for 30 seconds. Breathe steadily — don't hold your breath.
If you're shaking — that's correct. If your back is arching — you've gone too far.
Extend legs further from your body to make it harder; pull knees in to scale it down
Don't let the lower back arch up — that's your hip flexors taking over from your abs. Pull the belly button down.
Plank with Reach
3 × 10 each side
+
1
Get into a high plank — arms straight, hands under shoulders, body rigid.
2
Brace hard, then slowly extend one arm straight forward to shoulder height.
3
Hold for one second, return the hand, then alternate sides. That's one rep each.
The challenge is zero hip rotation — brace before you reach, not after
Move slowly — fast reps let you cheat through rotation without noticing
Don't shift your weight to the opposite side as you reach — stay centered and let the core do the work
Pike Push Up
3 × 10
+
1
Start in downward dog — hips high, forming an inverted V with your body.
2
Bend your elbows and lower your head toward the floor between your hands.
3
Press back up through your shoulders. Hips stay high the entire time.
Increasing to 10 reps here — same form as before, just more volume. Don't rush it.
The higher your hips, the more shoulder work. Keep them elevated.
Don't let your elbows flare wide — they should track directly over your wrists
Tuck-Up
3 × 12
+
1
Lie flat on your back, arms overhead, legs straight. This is your start position.
2
In one movement, crunch your knees toward your chest while your arms swing forward — meet in the middle.
3
Lower slowly back to the start position. That's one rep. Control the return.
This is a dynamic core movement — the controlled return is where most of the work happens
Aim for your hands to meet your knees at the same time — not one then the other
Don't flop back down — the slow lowering phase is half the exercise. Don't skip it.

Day 2 — Squat & Hinge

Pause Squat
4 × 12
+
1
Set up in your normal squat stance. Descend slowly to the bottom position.
2
Pause at the bottom for 2 full seconds. Stay braced — don't relax into the bottom.
3
Drive through your heels to stand. Squeeze glutes at the top.
The pause eliminates elastic energy — you have to generate the power yourself from a dead stop
Count the 2 seconds out loud or in your head — don't cheat yourself
Don't let your chest cave forward in the pause position — keep it up and brace your core
Single Leg Glute Bridge
3 × 12 each
+
1
Lie on your back, one foot flat near your hip, the other leg extended straight up.
2
Drive your hips up using the planted foot only. Squeeze the glute hard at the top.
3
Lower with control. Complete all reps on one side, then switch.
If your hips rotate or drop on the non-working side, your glutes aren't firing properly — slow down
Drive through your heel, not your toes
Don't let the extended leg drop — keep it vertical and stable throughout every rep
Bulgarian Split Squat
3 × 8 each
+
1
Place your rear foot on a chair or step behind you. Stand far enough that your front knee won't travel past your toes.
2
Lower your back knee toward the floor. Keep your torso upright throughout.
3
Drive through your front heel to return to standing. Complete all reps, then switch legs.
This is harder than a regular lunge — expect your front leg to shake. That's normal.
The higher the rear foot elevation, the harder the stretch on the hip flexor
Don't lean forward as you fatigue — torso stays upright the whole way. Leaning is cheating the movement.
Good Morning
3 × 15
+
1
Hands behind your head, slight bend in your knees. Stand tall before each rep.
2
Hinge at your hips — push them back, not down. Back stays flat.
3
Drive hips forward to return to standing. Feel the hamstrings load on the way down.
Increasing from 12 to 15 reps — still a hip hinge, not a squat. Hips go back.
Slow the descent down to feel the hamstring stretch — that's the whole point
Don't round your back — if you feel your spine curve, you've gone too far. Reduce the range.
Single Leg Calf Raise
3 × 15 each
+
1
Stand on one foot. Use a wall for balance but put minimal weight through your hand.
2
Rise as high as possible onto the ball of your foot. Pause at the top.
3
Lower slowly — 2 full seconds on the way down. Full stretch at the bottom before the next rep.
Single leg is dramatically harder than bilateral — expect to shake. Control is the priority, not speed.
The slow lower is where calf strength is actually built — don't rush it
Don't let your ankle roll outward as you fatigue — keep your weight centered through the big toe

Day 3 — Pull & Shoulders

Superman Rock
3 × 10
+
1
Lie face down in the superman position — arms overhead, chest and legs lifted.
2
Rock forward onto your chest slightly, then back onto your thighs. That's one rep.
3
Keep squeezing your glutes and upper back throughout the movement. Don't relax between reps.
This dynamic version builds more posterior chain endurance than the static hold
Stay tight — the rocking should feel controlled, not floppy
Don't lose height as you rock — if your arms or legs are dropping, you've relaxed. Reset and go again.
Arch Body Rock
3 × 10
+
1
Lie face down, lift into a full arch position — arms overhead, chest and legs up.
2
Rock forward onto your chest, then back onto your thighs. One smooth motion.
3
Maintain the arch through every rock — don't let the position collapse between reps.
This is a fundamental gymnastics conditioning drill — used at every level of the sport
Squeeze your glutes the entire time — they're holding the leg lift
Don't rock so far forward that your legs drop — the whole body should stay in the arch position throughout
Prone Y-T-W
3 × 10 each
+
1
Lie face down. Raise into Y (arms overhead in a V) — lift, squeeze 1 second, lower.
2
Raise into T (arms straight out to sides) — lift, squeeze 1 second, lower.
3
Raise into W (elbows bent like a flex) — lift, squeeze 1 second, lower. 10 reps each letter.
Increasing to 10 reps — add the 1-second squeeze at the top of every rep this week
Shoulder blades pinch together at the top of every position — that's the whole point
Don't rush through letters — complete all 10 of one before moving to the next. Don't mix them up.
Table Row
4 × 10
+
1
Lie under a sturdy table, grip the edge, body straight like a reverse plank.
2
Pull your chest up to the table — squeeze your shoulder blades hard at the top.
3
Lower with full control over 2 seconds. Don't let yourself drop.
Adding a 4th set this week — add a 1-second squeeze at the top of every rep
The slower the lower, the more pulling strength you're building
Don't let your hips sag as you fatigue — keep the body rigid throughout every set
Planche Lean
3 × 15 sec
+
1
Get into a high plank position. Hands turned slightly outward, fingers pointing to the sides.
2
Lean your whole body forward — shift your shoulders past your wrists.
3
Hold the lean. Push the floor away hard. Squeeze glutes and core. Hold for 15 seconds.
This trains the straight-arm shoulder strength that underlies every gymnastic push skill
The further forward you lean, the harder it gets — start small and increase lean over weeks
Don't let your elbows bend — arms must stay completely straight. If they bend, reduce the forward lean.
WEEK 5 & 6

Strength

Progressive overload Unlocked

Day 1 — Push & Core

Archer Push Up
3 × 6 each side
+
1
Get into a wide push up position — hands far apart, fingers pointing outward.
2
Lower toward one hand — that arm bends while the opposite arm stays straight and extended.
3
Press back up to center. That's one rep on that side. Alternate sides, or complete all on one then switch.
This is the direct progression toward a one-arm push up — the extended arm assists slightly while the working arm carries most of the load
Keep your body rigid — don't rotate toward the working side
Don't let the straight arm collapse — it stays extended the whole time, even when the other arm is bent
Hollow Body Rock
3 × 15
+
1
Get into your hollow body position — lower back pressed down, shoulders and legs raised.
2
Rock forward onto your shoulder blades, then back — maintaining the hollow shape throughout.
3
15 smooth rocks. Don't let the position break as you fatigue.
The rock is passive — generated by the position, not by kicking. Your body is the shape, the momentum does the rocking.
If your back arches during the rock — bend your knees. Quality of shape beats range of motion.
Don't let the lower back arch as you rock backward — the whole point is maintaining that lower back contact
Elevated Pike Push Up
3 × 8
+
1
Place your feet on a raised surface — chair, step, or couch. Hips up in a pike position, hands on floor.
2
Bend your elbows and lower your head toward the floor.
3
Press back up through your shoulders. The higher your feet, the more it resembles a handstand push up.
Elevating the feet dramatically increases shoulder load — this is the bridge between pike push ups and handstand push ups
Walk your feet up higher over weeks to progressively increase the load
Don't let your hips drop as you lower — keep them elevated in the pike the entire time
Plank Leg Lift
3 × 10 each
+
1
Get into a forearm plank. Body in a rigid straight line.
2
Brace hard, then lift one leg 6 inches off the floor — squeeze your glute to do it.
3
Hold for 1 second, lower, alternate sides. Keep hips level throughout.
Lift is generated by glute contraction — don't swing the leg or use momentum
Watch that your hip on the lifted side doesn't rotate up — keep the pelvis level
Don't lift too high — 6 inches is enough. High lifts cause the lower back to take over.
Tuck-Up
3 × 15
+
1
Lie flat, arms overhead, legs straight. Starting position.
2
Crunch your knees to your chest while your arms swing forward — meet in the middle.
3
Lower slowly back to start. Control the descent completely — don't let gravity do it for you.
Increasing to 15 reps — same quality. If you're rushing, slow down and drop to 12.
The return phase should take 2 full seconds — count it
Don't use momentum from your arms swinging to pull yourself up — the abs should initiate the crunch

Day 2 — Squat & Hinge

Assisted Pistol Squat
3 × 6 each
+
1
Stand facing a door frame or pole. Hold it lightly with one hand for balance. Extend one leg forward.
2
Sit back and down on one leg — keep your heel on the floor and chest upright.
3
Drive through your heel to stand. Use as little assistance from your hand as possible.
This is the hardest movement introduced in the program — don't rush it. Partial range is fine at first.
Your heel must stay on the floor — if it rises, your ankle mobility needs work. Elevate your heel slightly.
Don't rely heavily on the support — wean yourself off it. The hand is for balance, not for pulling yourself up.
Nordic Curl (Assisted)
3 × 5
+
1
Kneel on a cushion. Anchor your feet under a couch or have someone hold them. Hands ready to catch you.
2
Keeping your body straight from knee to shoulder, slowly lower yourself toward the floor. Control the descent.
3
Use your hands to catch yourself, push off slightly, then use your hamstrings to curl back up.
The lowering phase is the entire point — go as slowly as possible. This builds extreme hamstring strength.
5 reps is not a lot — each rep should be brutally slow and controlled
Don't hinge at the hips — your body must stay straight. Hinging removes the hamstring load completely.
Bulgarian Split Squat
4 × 10 each
+
1
Rear foot elevated on a chair. Front foot forward enough that your knee won't go past your toes.
2
Lower your back knee toward the floor. Torso stays upright — don't lean forward.
3
Drive through your front heel to stand. All reps on one side, then switch.
Adding a 4th set and 2 more reps this week — the same form applies. If form breaks, drop to 8.
By now this should feel more familiar. Increase your range of motion if the form is solid.
Don't let your front knee collapse inward on the way up — that's your glutes not firing. Drive that knee out.
Jump Squat
3 × 8
+
1
Stand in squat stance. Descend to a quarter squat position.
2
Explode upward — jump as high as possible. Arms can swing to assist.
3
Land softly with bent knees — absorb through your legs, not your joints. Reset and repeat.
Power training — your legs need to generate force, not just sustain it. This is a different quality than squats.
Land as quietly as possible — quiet landings mean soft, absorbed landings. Gymnastic landings.
Don't collapse on landing — control the deceleration. A crash landing is wasted athletic training.
Single Leg Calf Raise on Step
3 × 15 each
+
1
Stand with the ball of one foot on the edge of a step. Heel hanging below the step level.
2
Rise up onto your toes as high as possible. Full contraction at the top.
3
Lower slowly past neutral — let the heel dip below the step for a full stretch before the next rep.
The step allows a deeper stretch than floor calf raises — this extended range builds more strength
3 seconds down, 1 second up — slow negatives build the most calf strength
Don't skip the bottom stretch — partial range calf raises don't build the same capacity. Go all the way down.

Day 3 — Pull & Shoulders

Table Row — Feet Elevated
4 × 10
+
1
Set up your table row but place your feet on a chair so your body is horizontal.
2
Pull your chest up to the table — body stays completely rigid.
3
Lower with 2-second control. This is significantly harder than the floor version.
Elevating the feet shifts more of your bodyweight onto your arms — closer to a real pull up
Drive elbows back and down — not just "pull with your arms"
Don't let your hips sag — the elevated feet make it harder to maintain the line. Squeeze glutes throughout.
Superman Hold
3 × 30 sec
+
1
Lie face down, arms extended overhead, legs straight.
2
Lift arms, chest, and legs simultaneously. Squeeze your entire posterior chain.
3
Hold for 30 seconds. Breathe steadily. Don't drop between breaths.
Increasing to 30 seconds — if the position breaks before 30, rest 5 seconds and finish the hold
Lift from your upper back — don't just crane your neck
Don't only lift your arms — arms, chest, and legs must all be up equally. Half a superman builds half the muscle.
Wall Handstand Kick-Up
5 attempts
+
1
Place hands 6–8 inches from a wall. Kick one leg up and follow with the other — both feet against the wall.
2
Once up, push through your shoulders (shrug up). Squeeze your core and glutes. Hold as long as you can.
3
Lower one leg at a time. Rest 60 seconds. Repeat for 5 total attempts.
This is your first exposure to being upside down under load — it will feel unfamiliar. That's the point.
Push your shoulders into your ears — this is the key handstand cue and it starts here
Don't banana your lower back — squeeze your core and glutes so your body forms one straight line against the wall
Shoulder Tap Plank
3 × 15 each
+
1
High plank — arms straight, hands under shoulders, body in a rigid line.
2
Brace hard, lift one hand, tap the opposite shoulder. Return the hand.
3
Alternate for 15 reps each side. Zero hip rotation — that's the whole challenge.
Increasing to 15 reps — if hips are rotating, slow down significantly. Speed is the enemy here.
Widen your feet if you're rotating — a wider base makes anti-rotation easier
Don't rush through the reps to finish — each tap should be slow, deliberate, and rotation-free
L-Sit Tuck Attempt
3 × 10 sec
+
1
Sit on the floor. Place hands beside your hips, fingers pointing forward.
2
Press down through your hands and lift your hips off the floor. Tuck your knees toward your chest.
3
Hold for 10 seconds. Shoulders stay depressed — don't let them rise to your ears.
This is the foundation of the L-sit — the tuck version is where everyone starts. Build time here before extending the legs.
Push the floor away as hard as possible — active shoulder depression is what makes this work
Don't let your shoulders shrug up — actively push them down. Shrugging means your triceps are doing the work instead of your lats.

Peak

Day 1 — Push & Core

Archer Push Up
3 × 8 each side
+
1
Wide push up position, hands turned slightly outward. One arm bends, the other stays locked straight.
2
Lower toward the working side hand — the extended arm provides minimal assistance.
3
Press back to center. 8 reps each side. This is close to a one-arm push up.
By week 7 you should be able to reduce assistance from the extended arm — try going lighter on that contact
Full range — chest to floor on every rep. No partial reps this late in the program.
Don't twist your body toward the working arm — stay square. The core is working as hard as the chest here.
Hollow Body Hold
3 × 45 sec
+
1
Lower back pressed into the floor. Arms overhead, legs raised. This position should feel familiar.
2
Hold for 45 seconds. Breathe steadily throughout — don't hold your breath.
3
If form breaks before 45 seconds, rest briefly and restart rather than holding a broken position.
You've built to 45 seconds. This is elite-level hollow body endurance for a beginner. Earn it.
Extend your legs out further from your body to increase difficulty — less bend = more load
Don't just survive the 45 seconds in a bad position — if your back arches, the exercise is doing nothing
Wall Handstand Hold
3 × 20 sec
+
1
Kick up against the wall. Push shoulders hard into your ears. Squeeze core and glutes.
2
Hold for 20 seconds. Focus on keeping a straight line from hands to feet — no banana back.
3
Come down one leg at a time. Rest 90 seconds between holds.
Your shoulder endurance and core control are now strong enough for extended inverted holds — this is the payoff
Try pressing your heels toward the ceiling — this cue helps straighten the body line
Don't arch your lower back to stay up — brace your core hard. Arching is a balance compensation, not a real handstand.
Elevated Pike Push Up
4 × 10
+
1
Feet elevated on a chair or higher surface. Hips as high as possible in the pike.
2
Lower your head to the floor between your hands — elbows track over wrists.
3
Press through your shoulders to full lockout. 4 sets of 10 — this is significant shoulder volume.
By now this should feel controlled. Increase foot elevation if you want more challenge.
Full lockout at the top every single rep — partial lockout builds partial strength
Don't let the hips drop as you fatigue — fight to keep them elevated the entire set
L-Sit Tuck Hold
3 × 15 sec
+
1
Hands on the floor beside hips. Press down hard and lift your hips.
2
Tuck your knees up. Try to extend one leg out at a time if you can — progress toward the full L-sit.
3
Hold for 15 seconds. Shoulders stay depressed, elbows straight.
Attempt to extend one leg at a time — even holding a half-L for 5 seconds counts as progress toward the full skill
Push the floor away as hard as you can — more pushing force = more clearance from the floor
Don't let your elbows bend — the moment they bend, you lose the pressing structure needed to hold the position

Day 2 — Squat & Hinge

Pistol Squat Progression
3 × 6 each
+
1
Use minimal support — fingertips on a wall or the back of a chair. One leg extended forward.
2
Sit to your full depth. Work toward getting your hamstring touching your calf at the bottom.
3
Drive through your heel to stand. Reduce assistance compared to week 5-6.
By week 7, you should need significantly less support than when you started these — trust your legs
If full depth isn't there yet, that's fine — go to your current end range and it'll come
Don't let your standing heel come off the floor — if it does, work on ankle mobility separately and elevate your heel slightly
Nordic Curl
3 × 5
+
1
Kneel with feet anchored. Body straight from knee to shoulder. Hands ready to catch.
2
Lower as slowly as possible — use your hamstrings to fight gravity the entire way down.
3
Catch yourself at the bottom. Use minimal push from hands to return. Your hamstrings pull you back up.
By week 7 you should be able to lower more slowly and use less hand assistance on the way back up
Each rep slower than the last — the lowering is everything in this exercise
Don't hinge at the hips to make it easier — body must stay rigid from knee to shoulder throughout
Bulgarian Split Squat
4 × 12 each
+
1
Rear foot elevated. Set up in your established position — front foot far enough forward.
2
Lower to full depth — rear knee as close to the floor as possible. Torso stays vertical.
3
Drive through your front heel with power. 12 reps each side, 4 sets. This is peak volume for this movement.
4 sets of 12 is serious unilateral volume — this is where your single-leg strength becomes a real asset
Rest 90 seconds between sets — you've earned it. Don't rush this one.
Don't reduce depth to get through the reps — full range at lower reps is always better than partial range at higher reps
Broad Jump
3 × 5
+
1
Stand with feet shoulder-width. Load into a quarter squat, swinging arms back.
2
Explode forward — drive off both feet and swing your arms forward aggressively. Jump as far as possible.
3
Land softly in a controlled squat position. Absorb through your legs. Reset and repeat.
Power is the goal — full effort every jump. 5 reps should feel like 5 maximal attempts.
Land like a gymnast — quiet, controlled, stable. Your landing is as important as your takeoff.
Don't land stiff-legged — your knees and hips absorb the landing. Stiff landings put all the force into your joints.
Single Leg Calf Raise on Step
3 × 20 each
+
1
Ball of one foot on the step edge. Heel below the step. Other foot crossed behind.
2
Rise fully onto the toe. Hold 1 second at the top.
3
Lower all the way down — heel drops below the step for a full stretch. 20 reps with control.
20 full-range reps per side — by now this should feel genuinely challenging throughout every set

Day 3 — Pull & Shoulders

Typewriter Table Row
3 × 8
+
1
Set up your elevated table row. Pull your chest up to the table.
2
At the top, shift your body laterally toward one hand — then across to the other side, like a typewriter.
3
Return to center and lower. That's one rep. The lateral shift is the hardest part of this variation.
This is the hardest row variation in the program — it builds unilateral pulling strength without any equipment
Move slowly through the lateral — don't swing side to side. Controlled, deliberate movement.
Don't drop your hips as you shift laterally — the body stays rigid throughout the entire movement
Arch Body Hold
3 × 40 sec
+
1
Lie face down, arms overhead. Lift into your arch position — arms, chest, and legs all off the floor.
2
Hold for 40 seconds. Squeeze glutes and upper back throughout — don't let the position sag.
3
If you drop before 40 seconds, rest 10 seconds and continue. Work up to an unbroken hold.
40 seconds is advanced for this hold — you've built the posterior chain to earn this over 8 weeks
Arms overhead (hardest) — by your sides (easier). Challenge yourself with the harder variation if you can.
Don't only hold your arms up — the legs must be elevated equally. Half an arch hold builds half the muscle.
Wall Handstand Hold
3 × 30 sec
+
1
Kick up against the wall. Push shoulders into your ears. Squeeze everything — core, glutes, legs together.
2
Hold for 30 seconds. Try to take one or both feet off the wall briefly — begin developing balance.
3
Come down controlled. 90 seconds rest. 3 total holds.
30 seconds is a real handstand hold — by week 8 you should feel stable and in control up there
Try coming slightly off the wall to challenge your balance — this is where the freestanding handstand starts
Don't rely on the wall as a crutch — use it as a safety net, not a support. The goal is to hold without touching it.
L-Sit Tuck Hold
3 × 15 sec
+
1
Hands on floor, press down hard, lift hips. Tuck knees toward chest.
2
Work on extending one or both legs — even for 3–5 seconds. That's the full L-sit.
3
Hold for 15 seconds minimum in tuck. Push toward leg extension within that time.
By week 8 many people can hold a partial or full L-sit — attempt the leg extension every set
Lean forward slightly — shifting weight over the hands gives you more clearance for the legs
Don't give up on the leg extension just because it's hard — even a 2-second extended hold is meaningful progress
Prone Y-T-W
3 × 12 each

Lie face down. Y position — lift, squeeze 2 seconds, lower.
2
T position — lift, squeeze 2 seconds, lower.
3
W position — lift, squeeze 2 seconds, lower. 12 reps each, with the 2-second squeeze throughout.
12 reps with a 2-second squeeze — by now your upper back should be noticeably stronger than week 1
Quality over speed — these are finishers for a reason. Every rep done right.
